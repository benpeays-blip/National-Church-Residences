Typical Tech Stack for Large Nonprofits
1. Constituent Relationship Management (CRM)

This is the nonprofit’s core donor database — it tracks gifts, pledges, events, moves management, and communications history.

Most Common Platforms:

Blackbaud Raiser’s Edge NXT – industry standard for large nonprofits and faith-based foundations.

Salesforce Nonprofit Success Pack (NPSP) – flexible, cloud-based CRM for integrated donor + volunteer + grant tracking.

Virtuous CRM – growing in popularity for mid-to-large orgs seeking donor personalization and automation.

EveryAction / Bonterra – often used by advocacy or membership-based nonprofits.

Pain Points:

Limited integration with other tools.

Manual data entry for events or grants.

Reporting requires exports to Excel or Power BI.

2. Wealth Screening & Prospect Research Tools

Used to identify, score, and prioritize high-capacity donors and institutions.

Common Tools:

WealthEngine – wealth and lifestyle indicators.

iWave – combines wealth, philanthropic, and corporate data.

DonorSearch – major gift indicators and giving history across databases.

Windfall – real estate and household income data updated weekly.

Typical Use Case:

Quarterly or annual screenings uploaded into CRM for gift officer follow-up.

Pain Points:

Static data quickly becomes outdated.

Systems often siloed from CRM (manual import/export).

3. Marketing & Donor Communication Platforms

These tools manage email, newsletters, text campaigns, and donor journeys.

Common Platforms:

Mailchimp or Constant Contact – traditional email marketing.

HubSpot for Nonprofits – integrated marketing automation and donor nurturing.

Classy or Funraise – donation pages, crowdfunding, and event management.

Bloomerang + HubSpot – common pairing for donor engagement automation.

Pain Points:

Disconnected from CRM, so donor segmentation and personalization are limited.

No unified record of donor communications across channels.

4. Event Management Tools

Used for galas, luncheons, golf tournaments, or donor visits.

Common Platforms:

Eventbrite, Greater Giving, GiveSmart, or OneCause – registration, ticketing, and auction management.

Cvent – used for large national conferences or hybrid events.

Pain Points:

Event data (attendees, sponsors, engagement) often not synced back to CRM.

Limited insights on event ROI or attendee-to-donor conversion.

5. Grant Management Systems (GMS)

Tracks proposals, deadlines, submissions, and reporting for institutional funders.

Common Tools:

Fluxx – large foundations and government grantors.

Foundant GLM – common in mid-sized nonprofits managing multiple grants.

SmartSimple or Submittable – used for multi-funder coordination and workflow automation.

Pain Points:

Operates separately from donor CRM.

No centralized view of funder engagement, proposals, and outcomes.

6. Financial & Accounting Systems

Used for accounting, reconciliation, and integration with the fundraising database.

Common Tools:

Blackbaud Financial Edge NXT – integrates tightly with Raiser’s Edge.

QuickBooks Enterprise / Online Advanced – still common for nonprofits under $50M.

Sage Intacct – modern cloud-based accounting and budgeting platform.

Pain Points:

Manual reconciliation between accounting and development systems.

Revenue coding and fund designations can be inconsistent.

7. Data Analytics & Reporting Tools

Visualize performance, donor trends, and campaign results.

Common Tools:

Microsoft Power BI – highly adopted for dashboarding.

Tableau – popular for visual analytics and executive reporting.

Google Data Studio / Looker – for lightweight integrations.

Pain Points:

Requires manual data exports and cleaning.

Inconsistent data definitions across departments.

8. Collaboration & Project Management Tools

Used for internal communication, workflow tracking, and coordination across teams.

Common Tools:

Microsoft Teams / SharePoint – widely used for communication and document storage.

Asana / Monday.com / ClickUp – project tracking and collaboration.

Google Workspace – email, Drive, Docs, Sheets for shared collaboration.

Pain Points:

Fragmented communication between departments.

Files and data stored inconsistently (multiple versions, lack of control).

9. Digital Fundraising & Payment Processing

Used for online giving, recurring donations, and integrations with CRMs.

Common Platforms:

Classy, Funraise, or Givebutter – modern online giving solutions.

Stripe, Authorize.net, or PayPal Giving Fund – payment processing layers.

Double the Donation / 360MatchPro – employer matching integration.

Pain Points:

Donation data not always linked cleanly to CRM.

Limited donor journey tracking post-gift.

10. HR, Volunteer & Operations Systems

Manage HR, volunteers, and internal staff operations.

Common Tools:

Paylocity / ADP / Paycor – payroll and HR.

VolunteerHub / Galaxy Digital / Better Impact – volunteer tracking.

Smartsheet – used for operational planning and reporting.

Pain Points:

Volunteer and HR systems typically isolated from donor and grant systems.

Hard to connect operational impact to fundraising messaging.
