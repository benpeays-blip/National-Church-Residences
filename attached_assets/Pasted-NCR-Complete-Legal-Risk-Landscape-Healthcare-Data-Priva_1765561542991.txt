NCR – Complete Legal & Risk Landscape
Healthcare & Data Privacy
HIPAA – Protects resident health data; violations include unauthorized access, improper sharing, or lack of encryption.

HITECH Act – Expands HIPAA enforcement; requires breach notifications.

42 CFR Part 2 – Strict rules for handling addiction treatment records; violations include unauthorized disclosure.

CMS Regulations – Require accurate billing, reporting, and compliance; violations include false claims or inaccurate reporting.

State Health Privacy Laws – Residents may request access, correction, or deletion of personal data.

Housing & Resident Rights
Fair Housing Act – Prohibits discrimination in housing eligibility decisions; violations include biased AI recommendations.

HUD Regulations – Service coordinators must collect accurate data and report compliance.

Section 504 (Rehabilitation Act) – Requires accessibility for residents with disabilities.

ADA – Requires accessible technology and housing facilities.

State Senior Housing Licensing Laws – Regulate assisted living, memory care, and affordable housing.

Donor & Fundraising Compliance
IRS Form 990 – Requires accurate reporting of donations, grants, and expenses.

State Charitable Solicitation Laws – Require registration and disclosure when soliciting donations.

FASB/GAAP Standards – Require accurate financial reporting.

Grant Compliance Requirements – Funders require outcome reporting tied to specific metrics.

Donor Privacy Laws – Require protection of donor data.

Legal & Compliance
Corporate Entity Tracking – NCR must maintain accurate records for 1,100+ legal entities.

HUD/CMS Filing Requirements – Require timely, accurate compliance filings.

Sarbanes‑Oxley Principles – Require accurate financial controls and reporting.

State Nonprofit Corporation Laws – Govern board governance, fiduciary duties, and reporting.

Finance & Operations
IRS & GAAP Standards – Require accurate consolidated financial reporting.

Value‑Based Care Models – Require accurate outcome tracking tied to reimbursement.

Labor Laws (FLSA, OSHA) – Require proper overtime, breaks, and safe staffing ratios.

ERISA – Governs staff benefits.

State Employment Laws – Cover wage, hour, and workplace safety requirements.

Cybersecurity & Technology
HIPAA Security Rule – Requires safeguards for electronic health information.

State Data Breach Notification Laws – Require notifying residents and regulators of breaches.

FTC Act (Section 5) – Prohibits unfair or deceptive practices.

CISA Guidelines – Healthcare considered “target rich, cyber poor.”

PCI DSS – Governs online rent and donation payments.

Ethics & Trust
Bias in AI Decisions – Housing eligibility, donor scoring, and grant prioritization must be bias‑free.

Consent Management – Residents and donors must know how their data is used.

Transparency Requirements – Families and donors must see how outcomes are calculated.

Accessibility Standards (WCAG 2.1) – Apps and portals must be accessible.

Executive Takeaway
The full risk landscape for NCR includes:

Healthcare: HIPAA, HITECH, 42 CFR Part 2, CMS, state privacy laws.

Housing: Fair Housing Act, HUD, Section 504, ADA, state licensing.

Fundraising: IRS 990, solicitation laws, GAAP/FASB, grant compliance, donor privacy.

Legal: Entity tracking, HUD/CMS filings, Sarbanes‑Oxley principles, state nonprofit laws.

Finance: IRS, GAAP, value‑based care, labor laws, ERISA.

Cybersecurity: HIPAA Security Rule, breach laws, FTC Act, CISA, PCI DSS.

Ethics: Bias, consent, transparency, accessibility (WCAG).
